Our School

Founded on October 4, 1954, by the defunct Government of Eastern Nigeria, Queen's School Enugu stands as a premier institution for girls' education. Under the leadership of its first Principal, Mrs. Evelyn Doris Mather, the school quickly established a tradition of academic excellence, strict discipline, and motherly care. Starting with just 30 students, Queen's School grew into a center of excellence, offering a well-rounded education with modern facilities

Despite challenges such as the Nigerian Civil War, Queen's School has maintained its legacy of excellence in academics, sports, and the arts. The school continues to uphold its motto, "LABORARE UNO

ANIMO" (Working with One Mind), fostering unity, tolerance, and cooperation among its diverse student body. Over its long history, Queen's School Enugu has produced high achievers across various fields, instilling in its students a strong sense of dignity, self-confidence, and a commitment to national integration. Supported by a dedicated Old Girls' Association, PTA, and other stakeholders, the school remains a treasured institution and a beacon of women's education in Nigeria.
